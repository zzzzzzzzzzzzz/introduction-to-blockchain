Blakley scheme

Brief description

The idea is that any n nonparallel \( (n - 1) \) dimensional hyperplanes intersect at a specific point. The secret may be encoded as any single coordinate of the point (in our app by first). Assume that we use \( (k, n) \) threshold scheme to share secret \( S = x_1 \), that is element of finite field of size \( P (0 < k <= n < P, S < P, P\ is\ prime) \). At first we generate random \( x_2, ... , x_k \). After that we generate matrix \( A \) (\( size = (n, k) \)). In app we use Pascal matrix to avoid the problem of low-rank and calculate the vector \( y \). Every participant is given a string of \( A \) and the corresponding component of \( y \) among the prime number (they define an equation). Given subset of \( k \) of these equations, we can find the vector \( x \). The secret is \( x_1 \) (first component of \( x \)).
