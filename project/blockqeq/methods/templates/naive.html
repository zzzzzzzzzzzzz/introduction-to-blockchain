Naive scheme

Brief description

Suppose a group of people need to share a password for a treasure chest in such a way, that only together they can open it when it will be necessary

They can just divide a password into several parts and give a part to a group member. However there is a problem. If the password has length n and every symbol is a digit in range from 0 to 9 then, it will be possible for some members of a group to make a coalition and use brute force method to find other parts of password. So this scheme is not safe. It is much better if parts of password could have the same length as the password itself. For example, one can generate \( m-1 \) (where m is a number of team members) random strings of length \( n \), where every symbol is a digit in range from 0 to 9 and create \( m \)-th as a difference of password and sum of previously generated \( m-1 \) strings by modulo 10.

Another problem appears: what if the presence of all group member should not be necessary for recovering the secret? What if all group members are agree, that treasure chest can be opened if ⅓ or ½ of group members needed? In this case \( (t,n) \) - threshold secret sharing schemes can be used. \( (t,n) \)-threshold scheme is the scheme in which the dealer gives each player a share in such a way that any group of t or more players can together reconstruct the secret but no group of fewer than t players can.
